# WONML Planned Aircraft List

This is the working blueprint-hunt list for future faction expansion.

Goal:
- at least `10 aircraft per country`
- enough variety to support the `10 campaign` structure
- mix of `fighters`, `recon`, `two-seaters`, and `heavy threats`
- ordered roughly from earlier-war to later-war usefulness

Notes:
- this is a production planning list, not a final balancing sheet
- some countries historically flew imported or license-built aircraft; those are acceptable for WONML
- Germany and Britain remain the first priority for actual implementation

## Germany
1. `Aviatik B.II` - early war observation / campaign opener
2. `Fokker E.III` - early standard fighter
3. `Albatros D.I` - early mid-war fighter step
4. `Albatros D.III` - stronger Western Front fighter
5. `Albatros D.V` - standard mid-war fighter
6. `Pfalz D.III` - alternate fighter line / ace encounter
7. `Rumpler C.IV` - armed reconnaissance plane
8. `Fokker Dr.I` - high-climb ace aircraft
9. `Fokker D.VII` - late-war elite fighter
10. `Gotha G.V` - heavy bomber threat

## Britain
1. `B.E.2c` - early reconnaissance / vulnerable campaign target
2. `Airco DH.1` - early pusher / current game tree
3. `Airco DH.2` - early fighter / current game tree
4. `Sopwith Pup` - agile mid-war fighter / current game tree
5. `Sopwith Camel` - iconic late-war fighter
6. `S.E.5a` - fast late-war fighter
7. `Bristol F.2B Fighter` - armed two-seater / observer threat
8. `Airco DH.4` - bomber / strike aircraft
9. `Airco DH.9` - heavier late-tree aircraft / current game tree
10. `Handley Page O/400` - heavy bomber / major campaign threat

## America
American WW1 aviation used many French and British aircraft, so the list mixes native and American-flown service types.

1. `Curtiss JN-4 Jenny` - early training / domestic identity aircraft
2. `Curtiss R-4` - early American reconnaissance type
3. `Nieuport 28 (US service)` - early American front-line fighter
4. `SPAD XIII (US service)` - main late-war fighter
5. `Airco DH.4 Liberty` - key American bomber / reconnaissance aircraft
6. `Salmson 2A2 (US service)` - observation / armed recon
7. `Sopwith Camel (US service)` - fighter variety
8. `Bristol F.2B (US service candidate)` - two-seat fighter option
9. `SE.5a (US service candidate)` - late-war fighter option if needed
10. `Martin MB-1` - very late heavy bomber / optional end-of-war or post-armistice-style threat

## Suggested Faction Flavor
- Germany: disciplined fighter ladder, recon pressure, heavy bomber raids
- Britain: varied fighter identity, strong two-seaters, heavy bomber prestige
- France: fast elegant fighters, strong reconnaissance and bomber support
- Italy: agile mountain-war flavor, fast recon aircraft, Caproni bomber identity
- Russia: mixed imported fleet plus Ilya Muromets as signature heavy threat
- America: late-entry fast fighters and Liberty/DH-4 identity
